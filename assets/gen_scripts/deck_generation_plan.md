# 78-card Forest Whispers Plan

Each line defines the upright scene and the controlled change for its reversed
partner. The pair shares the listed visual anchor. Every role word in the table
(`figure`, `traveler`, `rider`, `family`, `teacher`, `artisan`, and similar) names
an intentional human archetype. Animals are optional symbolic accents, not the
deck's protagonist system. Object-led cards remain object-led.

All 78 cards use one **Art Nouveau shoujo jewel tarot** language: elegant human
figures, refined manga linework, symmetrical ornamental borders, layered symbolic
vignettes, crisp dark-blue contours, clean cel shading and luminous powder blue,
ice white, blush pink, lavender and silver with one jewel-tone accent. Avoid chibi,
pin-up posing, photorealism, 3D rendering and muddy painterly texture. Use the
reference only for general craft qualities; never reproduce a named artist,
character or exact composition.

## Subject Rules To Review

1. **Object-led**: the object is large and unmistakable; human figures remain
   secondary when the card's symbol requires it.
   Applies to `13, 16, 17, 18, 19, 22, 29, 36, 42, 50, 52, 59, 64`.
2. **Human archetype**: roles use elegant anatomy, expressive gaze, costume,
   gesture and symbolic props. Avoid generic fantasy character design.
3. **Symbolic action**: virtues and abstract forces are shown through gesture,
   posture, gaze, object relationships and environment.
4. Upright and reversed always retain the same subject, costume, location, camera,
   props and palette. Reversed changes only behavior, obstruction or relationship.
5. Every Minor Arcana card must visibly include its original suit object and exact
   rank count: flowering staffs, porcelain cups, straight swords or jade discs.
6. Every card has a narrow double-line frame and blank title panels; generated text,
   pseudo-writing, logos and decorative glyphs are rejected.

### Locked Examples

- **Emperor**: crowned lion in a fur mantle, straight sword in paw, eyes closed and
  muzzle lifted toward the sky as if chosen by fate. Reversed: the same lion lowers
  the sword across the path and uses the mantle like a wall; authority becomes control.
- **Strength**: a great lion voluntarily lowers its head while a tiny mouse rests a
  paw against it. Reversed: the lion tenses and the mouse retreats; trust becomes force.
- **Justice**: a white ermine balances two leaf scales through a perfectly still
  stance beside a straight sword. Reversed: one paw quietly hides a weight.
- **Temperance**: a river otter passes water between two porcelain cups in one fluid
  movement. Reversed: the rhythm breaks and both cups overflow.
